Some Previous Personal Projects

Just a little showcase of
some of the projects I've worked on in the past.

Mission Control

For the final project in a graphics class, my team of four students created a visually immersive sci-fi alien environment in Unreal Engine 4. The goal was to design a creative game environment/landscape over the course of the semester. I contributed by implementing sound design, adjusting lighting, and creating atmospheric visual effects to enhance the otherworldly ambiance. One of my key contributions was developing a custom particle-based fog system in Unreal Engine. This fog dynamically spread and reformed as players moved through it, leaving a temporary visual trail reminiscent of real-life interactions with dense fog. This feature added a unique interactive element to the environment and helped guide exploration through the alien forest. The project highlighted my ability to combine technical and creative skills to deliver an engaging and immersive game world.

Nightmare At the Museum

For the final project in a graphics and game design class, I collaborated with a graphic design student and another developer to create a small FPS-style zombie horror game in Unity within a tight 1-3 week timeframe. My primary responsibilities included designing and implementing the game’s inventory system, weapon mechanics, player health system, and various in-game interactions like enemy pathfinding mechanics to ensure enemies could effectively follow and engage the player. I also developed the user interface (UI) elements to ensure an intuitive and immersive player experience. This project emphasized teamwork, rapid prototyping, and creative problem-solving to deliver a functional and engaging gameplay experience.
